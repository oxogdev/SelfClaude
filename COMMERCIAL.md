# Commercial Licensing

SelfClaude is distributed under the [Business Source License 1.1](./LICENSE). Most users — individual developers and small teams — never need to think about commercial licensing. This document is for the cases where you do.

## Who needs a commercial license?

You need a commercial license **if either** of the following applies to your use:

1. **More than 10 people in your organization use SelfClaude.** The free production-use grant in [LICENSE](./LICENSE) covers organizations with up to ten individuals using SelfClaude in any capacity. Larger teams (mid-size companies, enterprise IT, large open-source projects with ten-plus collaborators all running it) need a commercial license.
2. **You offer SelfClaude (or its functionality) to third parties as a hosted, managed, or embedded service.** Reselling SelfClaude as part of a SaaS, PaaS, or wrapper product that competes with the Licensor's commercial offerings always requires a commercial license, regardless of team size.

## Who does **not** need a commercial license?

Most uses are free under the BSL grant:

- Individual developers running it on their own machines.
- Teams of up to 10 people using it for internal development work.
- Anyone making non-production use (evaluating, learning, contributing patches).
- Open-source projects (provided ≤10 contributors actively use the tool).
- Modifications, derivative works, and redistribution under the same License.

After the **Change Date** (2030-05-08), all of the above restrictions disappear: SelfClaude becomes available under the Apache License 2.0 with no commercial-use restrictions. Until then, the BSL grant applies.

## What does a commercial license cover?

- **Unlimited internal users** within the licensed organization.
- **Production deployment** as part of internal developer tooling, CI/CD pipelines, or platform engineering setups.
- **Right to resell or embed** SelfClaude functionality into your own products (negotiated per-deal).
- **Priority support channel** (response SLA depends on tier).
- **Indemnification** for the licensed organization against IP claims (negotiated).

## Why BSL, not MIT?

Honest answer: SelfClaude is dev tooling that genuinely earns its keep at organizations that put real engineering throughput through it. MIT (the previous license) gave that value away for free; BSL keeps individual + small-team users free while creating a pathway for larger users to fund the project's continued development.

The **4-year sunset** (Change Date) ensures long-term open-source commitment: every release becomes Apache 2.0 four years after publication. This isn't open-core or rug-pull licensing — it's "fair source" with a guaranteed eventual return to permissive open source.
